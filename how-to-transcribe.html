How to transcribe audio & video
for free

By Yogev Shaked · 5 min read · Updated May 2025

Whether you recorded a university lecture, a Zoom meeting, a podcast, or a voice memo — turning it into searchable, editable text opens up a lot of possibilities. You can summarize it with AI, create study notes, share it with colleagues, or just search for a specific moment without re-watching the whole thing.

This guide covers the fastest, most accurate free way to do it in 2025, using a tool called Lectio that runs entirely on Google's infrastructure — so you pay nothing and install nothing.

Why transcribe audio and video?

Here are the most common reasons people want a transcript of a recording:

  • Study notes from lectures. Skim a 2-hour lecture in 10 minutes. Search for exactly the part you need.
  • AI summarization. Paste the transcript into ChatGPT, Claude, or NotebookLM and get a structured summary, flashcards, or answers to specific questions.
  • Zoom and meeting recordings. Turn a long team call into a searchable document with action items.
  • Accessibility. Make audio content readable for people who are deaf, hard of hearing, or just prefer reading.
  • Content repurposing. Turn a podcast episode or interview into a blog post or newsletter.

The best free method: Whisper AI

There are several free transcription tools available, but most have significant limitations — short time limits, file size caps, watermarked output, or poor accuracy on accented speech or non-English languages.

Whisper, released by OpenAI as an open-source model, is a different story. It consistently ranks among the most accurate transcription models available, handles 90+ languages including Hebrew and Arabic, and is completely free to use. The catch: running it well requires a GPU, which most computers don't have.

That's where Lectio comes in. It runs Whisper on Google Colab — Google's free cloud computing service that gives every Google user access to a free GPU. No subscription, no credit card, no install.

How free is it, really? Google Colab's free tier provides enough GPU time for most students and professionals. A 1-hour recording uses roughly 3–6 minutes of GPU time. You'd need to transcribe hundreds of hours of audio before hitting any limits.

How to transcribe audio or video for free — step by step

This works for any audio or video file: MP3, MP4, WAV, M4A, Zoom recordings, Google Meet recordings, voice memos, podcasts, and more. All you need is a free Google account.

  • 1

    Open the free notebook

    Click the "Start transcribing" button on Lectio's homepage. This opens a notebook on Google Colab — think of it as a free cloud computer that does all the work for you. No software to download or install.

  • 2

    Click "Run All"

    At the top of the Colab page, click Runtime → Run All (or press Ctrl+F9). This installs Whisper on the cloud machine — it only takes about 2 minutes the first time.

  • 3

    Upload your file

    A file picker will appear. Select your audio or video file from your computer. Files up to about 2 GB work fine. For larger recordings, there are instructions in the notebook for connecting Google Drive.

  • 4

    Wait a few minutes

    Whisper transcribes your file using Google's free GPU. A 1-hour recording takes roughly 3–6 minutes. You'll see a progress indicator as it runs.

  • 5

    Download your transcript

    A clean .txt file downloads automatically when transcription finishes. Open it in any text editor, paste it into Google Docs, or drop it straight into an AI tool.

Which languages are supported?

Whisper supports over 90 languages and can auto-detect the language of your recording. Languages with strong support include:

  • Hebrew (עברית) — including mixed Hebrew/English lectures
  • Arabic (العربية)
  • English
  • Russian (Русский)
  • French, Spanish, German, Italian, Portuguese
  • Japanese, Korean, Chinese (Mandarin)
  • Turkish, Polish, Dutch, and many more

If your recording mixes languages — for example a Hebrew lecture that uses English terminology — Whisper generally handles it well.

Tips for better transcription accuracy

Audio quality matters most

Whisper is remarkably robust, but cleaner audio always produces better results. If your recording has a lot of background noise, consider using a free tool like Audacity to reduce it before uploading.

One speaker at a time is easier than many

Transcription accuracy is highest when there's a single clear speaker. Panel discussions and group meetings with multiple overlapping voices are harder — Whisper will still try, but accuracy may be lower in those sections.

Specify the language if you know it

Auto-detection works well for most recordings, but if you know the language in advance, you can set it explicitly in the notebook. This can improve accuracy, especially for shorter recordings.

Large files take longer but still work

A 3-hour lecture will take roughly 10–15 minutes. The notebook will show progress — just leave the Colab tab open and come back when it's done.

Using your transcript with AI tools

The .txt file Lectio produces is a clean, plain-text transcript — no timestamps, no metadata, just the spoken words. This makes it immediately usable with any AI tool:

  • ChatGPT or Claude: Paste the transcript and ask for a summary, key points, a study guide, or answers to specific questions.
  • NotebookLM: Upload the transcript as a source. NotebookLM will index it so you can ask questions about the lecture directly.
  • Google Docs: Paste the transcript, then use Docs' built-in AI features to summarize or reformat.
  • Obsidian / Notion: Drop it into your notes for future reference and search.

Frequently asked questions

Is Lectio really free?
Yes, completely. It runs on Google Colab's free GPU tier — Google subsidises this for all Google account holders. Lectio itself has no server costs and charges nothing. There's a donate link if you want to support the project, but it's entirely optional.
Is my file private?
Your file is uploaded directly to your own Google Colab session — not to Lectio's servers (Lectio has no servers). Google deletes the session automatically when it ends. Lectio never sees your file.
Do I need to install anything?
No. Everything runs in your browser through Google Colab. The only requirement is a free Google account — which most people already have.
How long does transcription take?
Roughly 3–6 minutes per hour of audio on Colab's free GPU. The first time also takes about 2 minutes to install the Whisper model (one-time setup per session).
What's the maximum file size?
Google Colab can handle files up to about 2 GB directly. For larger files, you can connect your Google Drive and load the file from there — the notebook includes instructions.
How accurate is Whisper compared to other free tools?
Whisper large-v2 (the model Lectio uses) consistently outperforms most free alternatives, especially on non-English languages, accented speech, and technical vocabulary. It's the same underlying technology that powers many paid transcription services.

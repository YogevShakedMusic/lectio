How it works

Four steps,
no tech skills needed

Google Colab is just a free cloud computer. You don't need to understand it — just click.

1

Click "Start transcribing"

Opens a notebook on Google Colab — Google's free cloud service. No install needed, just a Google account.

2

Hit "Run All"

One click sets everything up. A file picker appears — choose your lecture video or audio file.

3

Wait a few minutes

Google's free GPU transcribes your file using state-of-the-art Whisper AI. A 1-hour lecture takes ~3 minutes.

4

Download your transcript

A clean .txt file downloads automatically — ready to paste into ChatGPT, Claude, NotebookLM, or any note-taking app.

Questions

FAQ

Do I need a Google account?
Yes, Google Colab requires a free Google account. If you use Gmail or Google Drive (most students do), you're already set.
Is my lecture recording private?
Your file is uploaded to your own Google Colab session — not to us. We never see it. When your Colab session ends, the file is deleted automatically.
How long does transcription take?
A 1-hour lecture takes 3–6 minutes on Colab's free GPU. The first run also installs the model (~2 minutes one-time setup).
What's the file size limit?
Google Colab handles files up to ~2GB directly. For larger files, you can connect your Google Drive — instructions are in the notebook.
Can I use the transcript with ChatGPT or NotebookLM?
That's exactly what it's built for. The output is a clean .txt file you can paste anywhere — ChatGPT, Claude, NotebookLM, Google Docs, wherever.
Why Colab instead of a regular website?
Running Whisper large-v2 requires a GPU, which costs real money to host. Colab gives every Google user free GPU time. This way I can offer the full-quality model, free, forever — without charging anyone or running ads.
